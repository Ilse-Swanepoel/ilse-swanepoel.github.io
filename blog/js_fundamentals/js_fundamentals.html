JavaScript Fundamentals


HTML, CSS and JS

In teaching, we often asked students questions about a text to see how much they understood from reading. The 2 main types were literal and inferential questions. Literal questions would be questions you can find in the text and inferential questions were a “read between the lines” type deal. Literal questions (IQ) could be answered by looking at the book. For example, what is Billy doing? “He is reading.” Inferential questions (EQ) were more complex and the children often referred to them as thinking questions. They had to look at different clues and figure out what was going. For example, how does Billy feel about reading? How do you know? “He hates it, look at his face. He looks grumpy and it says he sighed maybe he wants to go and do something else”.
